What Is EDI Automation?

Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) is the standardized electronic exchange of business documents — purchase orders, invoices, advance ship notices, and more — between trading partners. EDI automation replaces manual data entry and error-prone processes with intelligent, rules-based workflows that process these documents automatically.
‍

Crstl is an AI-native EDI and supply chain commerce platform that automates order-to-cash workflows between brands, retailers, and logistics partners. With Crstl, brands onboard new retail trading partners in days instead of months, automatically generate compliant ASNs and invoices, and connect seamlessly with Shopify, NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics 365, QuickBooks, Amazon, Walmart, Target, Costco, and hundreds more.

Your Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) About EDI Automation, Answered

What is Crstl?

Crstl is an AI-powered EDI and B2B agentic commerce platform that automates order-to-cash workflows between brands, retailers, and logistics providers. Our AI agent, Edison, processes purchase orders, generates ASNs, creates invoices, and ensures retailer compliance autonomously — handling over $1 billion in B2B GMV.

What is EDI automation?

EDI (Electronic Data Interchange) automation replaces manual document exchange between trading partners with software that automatically sends and receives standardized business documents like purchase orders (850), advance ship notices (856), and invoices (810). Crstl takes this further with AI agents that not only transmit documents but validate, reconcile, and resolve errors autonomously.

How long does EDI onboarding take with Crstl?

Most brands are fully onboarded and transacting with their retail trading partners within days or weeks, not the months typical of legacy EDI providers. Crstl handles all compliance testing and trading partner certification for you.

What retailers and trading partners does Crstl support?

Crstl supports all major retailers including Walmart, Target, Costco, Amazon, UNFI, Whole Foods, Kroger, and hundreds more. We handle the full range of EDI document types required by each retailer, including retailer-specific compliance requirements and routing guides.

How is Crstl different from legacy EDI providers?

Crstl is AI-native, built from the ground up with agentic automation. Some key differences: an AI agent (Edison) that resolves errors autonomously; full proprietary vendor compliance API access for developer teams; no per-document fees; and every account gets a dedicated implementation specialist and direct Slack access to the Crstl team.

Does Crstl integrate with NetSuite, Shopify, and other ERPs?

Yes. Crstl integrates with NetSuite, Shopify, Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central, QuickBooks, and other ERP and commerce platforms. These integrations enable fully automated order-to-cash workflows without manual data entry or CSV exports.

What EDI documents does Crstl support?

Crstl supports all standard EDI transaction sets in the 800- and 900-series including: 850 (Purchase Order), 855 (Purchase Order Acknowledgment), 856 (Advance Ship Notice / ASN), 810 (Invoice), 820 (Payment Order), 997 (Functional Acknowledgment), and dozens more. We also support custom document mappings for retailer-specific requirements.

Does Crstl charge per-document fees?

No. Unlike legacy EDI providers that charge per document or per kilocharacter, Crstl offers transparent, predictable pricing with unlimited EDI documents. This means your costs stay predictable as your order volume grows, with no surprise fees during peak seasons.

Can Crstl help reduce EDI chargebacks?

Yes. EDI chargebacks from retailers like Walmart and Target are typically caused by ASN errors, late shipments, or non-compliant labels. Crstl’s AI agent Edison validates every document against the retailer’s specific compliance rules before submission, catching errors that would otherwise result in chargebacks.

Is Crstl suitable for small businesses or only enterprise?

Crstl serves businesses of all sizes — from emerging DTC brands making their first retail shipment to enterprise companies managing hundreds of trading partners. For small businesses, we offer full-service EDI that requires no technical expertise. For developer teams at larger companies, we provide full API access and webhook integrations for custom workflows.
